What is IP Rating? (Complete Guide)

IP Rating (Ingress Protection Rating) is an international standard (IEC 60529) that indicates how well a smartphone, smartwatch, or other electronic device is protected against dust and water. In general, the higher the IP Rating, the better the device's protection.

An IP Rating consists of the letters "IP" followed by two numbers, such as IP54, IP65, IP67, IP68, and IP69.

The first digit indicates the level of protection against dust.

The second digit indicates the level of protection against water.

How to Read an IP Rating?

First Digit – Dust Protection

  • 0 – No protection against dust.
  • 5 – Limited dust may enter, but it will not affect normal operation.
  • 6 – Completely dustproof. No dust can enter the device.

Second Digit – Water Protection

  • 4 – Protected against water splashes.
  • 5 – Protected against low-pressure water jets.
  • 6 – Protected against powerful water jets.
  • 7 – Can withstand immersion in water up to 1 meter for about 30 minutes.
  • 8 – Can withstand deeper water for a specified period, depending on the manufacturer.
  • 9 – Protected against high-pressure and high-temperature water jets.

Advantages of IP Rating

  • ✔️ Better protection against dust.
  • ✔️ Protects the phone from rain and water splashes.
  • ✔️ Reduces the risk of damage if the phone is accidentally dropped into water.
  • ✔️ Improves the durability and reliability of the device.

  • ✔️ Ideal for outdoor activities, travel, and everyday use.
  • ✔️ A higher IP Rating is often a sign of premium build quality.

Limitations of IP Rating

Having an IP Rating does not mean that a phone is completely waterproof. There are several limitations to keep in mind.

  • ❌ Protection against salt water or chlorinated water is not guaranteed.
  • ❌ Water resistance may decrease if the phone is dropped or physically damaged.
  • ❌ Many manufacturers do not cover water damage under warranty.
  • ❌ IP Ratings are tested under controlled laboratory conditions, so real-world performance may vary.
  • ❌ The phone should not be intentionally submerged in water or used underwater for extended periods.

Common IP Ratings and Their Meanings

IP54

  • Limited dust protection.
  • Protected against water splashes.
  • Common in budget and mid-range smartphones.

IP65 / IP66

  • Completely dustproof.
  • Protected against powerful water jets.
  • Suitable for light rain and outdoor use.

IP67

  • Completely dustproof.
  • Can withstand immersion in water up to 1 meter for about 30 minutes.

IP68

  • Completely dustproof.
  • Can withstand deeper water for a limited time, depending on the manufacturer's specifications.
  • Commonly found in flagship smartphones.

IP69

  • Completely dustproof.
  • Protected against high-pressure and high-temperature water jets.
  • Considered one of the highest levels of standard IP protection available in smartphones.

Is IP68 Better Than IP67?

Yes. IP68 provides better water resistance than IP67. However, the actual level of protection depends on the manufacturer's testing conditions and specifications.

Is an IP-Rated Phone Completely Waterproof?

No. An IP Rating means the phone is water-resistant, not waterproof. You should avoid intentionally submerging the device in water or using it in swimming pools or seawater.

Conclusion

If you are planning to buy a new smartphone, checking its IP Rating is a smart decision. IP54 is suitable for everyday use, while IP67 and IP68 offer significantly better protection against dust and water. If you frequently travel, use your phone in rainy conditions, or want extra durability, choosing a smartphone with a higher IP Rating is recommended. However, even with an IP Rating, it is always best to protect your phone from unnecessary exposure to water and dust.
