What is Refresh Rate? (Complete Guide)

Introduction

Refresh Rate refers to the number of times a smartphone's display refreshes or updates the image on the screen every second. It is measured in Hertz (Hz).

For example:

  • 60Hz = The display refreshes 60 times per second.
  • 90Hz = The display refreshes 90 times per second.
  • 20Hz = The display refreshes 120 times per second.
  • 144Hz = The display refreshes 144 times per second.

The higher the Refresh Rate, the smoother animations, scrolling, and gaming experience will appear.

How Does Refresh Rate Work?

Imagine you're scrolling through social media or playing a game. On a 60Hz display, the screen updates 60 times every second, while a 120Hz display updates 120 times every second. As a result, movements, animations, and transitions appear much smoother and more fluid on a higher Refresh Rate display.

Advantages of Refresh Rate

  • ✔️ Smoother scrolling experience.
  • ✔️ Better app opening and closing animations.
  • ✔️ More fluid gaming experience.
  • ✔️ More natural and responsive touch interactions.
  • ✔️ Enhanced overall user experience.
  • ✔️ Better visual smoothness while watching supported content.

Benefits of Refresh Rate in Gaming

Refresh Rate has a direct impact on visual smoothness during gaming.

A higher Refresh Rate provides several gaming advantages:

  • ✔️ Smoother gameplay in games such as PUBG Mobile, BGMI, Call of Duty: Mobile, and Free Fire.
  • ✔️ Easier tracking of enemy movements.
  • ✔️ More fluid camera rotation.
  • ✔️ Better experience in fast-paced action games.
  • ✔️ Improved visual responsiveness during gameplay.

«Note: If a game supports only 60 FPS, a 120Hz display will not provide its full benefit. To experience smoother gameplay, both the display's Refresh Rate and the game's FPS support should match.»

Limitations of Refresh Rate

  • ❌ Higher Refresh Rates may consume more battery power.
  • ❌ Not all apps and games support high Refresh Rates.
  • ❌ A low-end processor may prevent you from fully benefiting from a high Refresh Rate display.

Common Refresh Rates

60Hz

  • Suitable for everyday use.
  • Ideal for basic tasks such as calling, messaging, browsing, and watching videos.

90Hz

  • Offers a noticeably smoother experience than 60Hz.
  • A good balance between smoothness and battery efficiency.

120Hz

  • Excellent for gaming and premium smartphones.
  • Provides smooth scrolling, animations, and gameplay.

144Hz

  • Designed for competitive gaming smartphones.
  • Delivers an ultra-smooth visual experience in supported games.

Which Refresh Rate is Best?

If your budget allows, a 120Hz display is the best choice for most users. It offers an excellent balance of smoothness, gaming performance, and battery life.

A 90Hz display is also a great option for everyday use and casual gaming, while a 60Hz display is generally suitable only for basic smartphone usage.

Conclusion

Refresh Rate is one of the most important display features that affects how smooth a smartphone feels during everyday use and gaming. A higher Refresh Rate provides smoother scrolling, better animations, and a more enjoyable gaming experience. For most users, 120Hz is the ideal choice, while 90Hz offers excellent value, and 60Hz remains suitable for basic tasks.
