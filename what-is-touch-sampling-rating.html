What is Touch Sampling Rate? (Complete Guide)

Introduction

Touch Sampling Rate indicates how many times a smartphone's screen detects your finger's touch in one second. It is measured in Hertz (Hz).

It is important not to confuse Touch Sampling Rate with Refresh Rate, as they are two different display specifications.

Refresh Rate = How many times the display updates its image every second.

Touch Sampling Rate = How many times the display detects your touch every second.

A higher Touch Sampling Rate results in faster touch recognition, lower input delay, and a more responsive user experience.

Examples

  • 120Hz Touch Sampling Rate = The screen detects touch 120 times per second.
  • 240Hz Touch Sampling Rate = The screen detects touch 240 times per second.
  • 480Hz Touch Sampling Rate = The screen detects touch 480 times per second.
  • 1000Hz or 2000Hz Instant Touch Sampling Rate = Provides extremely fast touch response, especially during gaming.

Which Touch Sampling Rate is Best?

If you play games such as BGMI, PUBG Mobile, Free Fire, or Call of Duty: Mobile, it is recommended to choose a smartphone with at least a 240Hz or 360Hz Touch Sampling Rate. These provide faster touch response and better control during gameplay.

For everyday activities like browsing, social media, video streaming, and messaging, a 120Hz Touch Sampling Rate is more than sufficient.

Conclusion

Touch Sampling Rate is an important display feature that determines how quickly a smartphone responds to your touch. A higher Touch Sampling Rate provides faster input detection, smoother interactions, and a better gaming experience. While 120Hz is suitable for regular users, 240Hz, 360Hz, or higher is recommended for gamers who want faster and more precise touch response.
